Lionel Aldridge

Lionel Aldridge was a professional football player who was diagnosed with schizophrenia at the age of 33. He had played the position of the all skyline tackle and co-captain as a college student at Utah State. For 11 years, he had played as a Green Bay Packer and San Diego charger. Before his retirement, he had been able to play two seasons with the San Diego Charger. Upon his retirement, he was a sports analyst until being diagnosed with paranoid schizophrenia. Paranoid schizophrenia is a sub-type of schizophrenia but one of the most common type of schizophrenia. Once his schizophrenic episodes had gotten worse as time went by, him and his wife, the mother of his two daughters, had signed divorce papers that had led him to becoming homeless for about two years. He had lost both of his super bowl rings when he was homeless on the streets. Aldridge had became mentally ill fighting schizophrenia on his own without treatment. After ten years of fighting the illness on his own, he finally accepted the treatment and had recuperated within a couple years. He started to speak in programs to help him through the final stages of schizophrenia. He was unbelievably grateful for his recovery and had finally felt free again after so many years. He continued to speak in meetings, spreading information about schizophrenia until his death in February 12, 1998
